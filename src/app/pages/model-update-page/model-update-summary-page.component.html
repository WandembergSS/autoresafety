Step 6 purpose and working format

Step 6 closes the workflow by consolidating the information produced in the five previous steps and preparing the final deliverable set. Instead of asking you to rewrite or duplicate data, this page resumes the analysis already performed and keeps the final stage focused on review, consistency checking, and artifact generation.

What Step 6 does

The goal of this step is to confirm that the scope definition, initial models, control structure, unsafe behaviors, controller constraints, loss scenarios, and safety requirements are coherent as one final safety case. Step 6 acts as the transition from analysis work to final project output.

How the substeps are organized

  • Review the resumed content of Steps 1 to 5.
  • Reopen any previous step when you need to correct or refine information.
  • Confirm that the previous analysis is complete enough for final packaging.
  • Generate the ReSafety artifacts from the full project payload.

Why this current format is used

This page is intentionally structured as a summary and artifact hub because the detailed modeling and analysis decisions already belong to the previous steps. Repeating full editing forms here would duplicate effort, increase the risk of inconsistency, and make the end of the workflow harder to validate. The current format gives you a controlled final checkpoint: first inspect the resumed state, then jump back to the exact step that needs adjustment, and finally generate the final artifacts only when the chain is complete.
